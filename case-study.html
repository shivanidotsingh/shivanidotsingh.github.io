A West Oakland Story

Context: West Oakland is going through urban development that's causing a significant impact on the existing population. The Crucible, a nonprofit industrial arts school at the heart of it, collaborated with us to visualize the impact of upcoming changes and build a story from the numbers to inform their five-year strategy.

1. Discovery

We started with secondary research to understand the wide scope of changes coming to West Oakland. The proposed developments promised new opportunities but also threatened the neighborhood's identity as a center for the arts and affordable living. One project alone, the Panoramic, was expected to bring over 2,000 new residents, posing a challenge to The Crucible's mission of serving an underserved community now at risk of displacement.

2. Development

We brainstormed various mediums to tell this story, from Legos and drones to podcasts. After much deliberation, we presented three core ideas to The Crucible: a layered map, a storyboard, and a playful game. The client was most drawn to the game idea, which could make complex, high-stakes information more approachable.

3. Delivery

We developed a prototype based on Monopoly, a fitting metaphor for the real-estate gentrification West Oakland was facing. Feedback on our first version was invaluable; we learned the gravity of the situation was being lost. We iterated by placing a rendering of the future 7th street at the center of the board and redesigned our "impact cards" to visually emphasize the hard-hitting consequences of gentrification.
